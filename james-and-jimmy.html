CONTEXT

Self-checkout kiosks have become common-place in Australian supermarkets over the past few years.

The overall experience, however, remains difficult for customers young and old.

CHALLENGE

Design and build a website capable of housing the many identities existent in James’ practice and life.

OUTCOME

Removing silos to display multiple content sections alongside each other leading to a playful yet refined site.

PROCESS

James wanted the site to playfully respond to the idea of multiple identities and perceptions.

Websites of architects that James admires are all immersive, somewhat challenging and reflect their attitude towards design.

For these reasons it essential that it also didn’t have a template feel to it leading me to research alternative ways of presenting the content structure, sketching ideas though mapping the IA and a series of wire-frames.

Wanting the site to engage users somehow I looked for ways to incorporate interaction, testing various JavaScript libraries and snippets of code through CodePen.

DESIGN

This project provided the challenge of creating the engaging site imagined whilst developing a site within my ability.

Adding just the right amount of JavaScript interaction allowed me to stay in control of the design.

The final design comprised of six sections — four nav bars and two content sections of 'Writing' and 'Projects' presented alongside each other, with equal weight and importance.

Intentionally overwhelming at first, the clean design and multiple navigation bars allowed users to find their bearings quickly, then flow through the site with ease.
