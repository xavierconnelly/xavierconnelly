CONTEXT

Self-checkout kiosks have become common-place in Australian supermarkets over the past few years.

The overall experience, however, remains difficult for customers young and old.

CHALLENGE

Redesign the self checkout experience with particular attention to the interface design.

OUTCOME

An updated loyalty program capable of providing a faster, more personalised experience for returning customers.

PROCESS

To understand how the current system and interface were performing the team conducted interviews at local supermarkets.

Transferring customer insights onto an affinity map, we then worked independently for the remainder of the project. Weighing and searching for non-barcoded items begun to stand out as the primary pain point for customers.

I decided to analyse the interface and map the IA hoping to make sense of the content structure and uncover why customers were having such difficulty.

With up to 24 items being displayed users were being overwhelmed and continuously scanning content as they transitioned between screens.

DESIGN

To counteract this issue I housed the items within a scrolling keyboard, reducing the number of items shown and hierarchical groups to enable users to remain present.

Central to the design was a personalised screen for loyalty customers.

Items from previous shops would be stored together, significantly reducing clutter to increase both user flow and brand loyalty.

NEXT STEPS

Looking at the wider service showed potential to house touch points within baskets and trolleys, bringing the interaction forward to when a customer enters a store.

This would ease a bottleneck of user flow during payment while allowing the opportunity for the store to target customers with personalised sales.
