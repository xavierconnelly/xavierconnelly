CONTEXT

Based in Lisbon, Portugal, PADA is a new artist residency founded mid way through 2018.

I joined shortly after, providing an array of design solutions from service design, UX/UI through to print.

CHALLENGE

To match and exceed residents’ expectations working within a limited budget and team.

OUTCOME

A collection of frameworks that enabled the owner-operators to maximise their time and focus on delivering a high level of customer service.

PROCESS

In order to uncover design opportunities, I immersed myself within PADA analysing the organisation through a variety of mapping tools.

Interviews conducted with residents highlighted the importance placed on the overall experience of being immersed within a foreign culture.

For many, this was a key motivator for taking part in a residency and was currently being overlooked.

The owners Tim and Diana had only just moved to Portugal themselves, creating a disconnect between the local knowledge they could provide and the expectations of the residents.

This was preventing the residents from engaging with the city to the extent they had hoped.

DESIGN

After thorough research I developed a series of guides to aid the residents exploring Lisbon, connecting to specialist directories where possible.

I opted for a paired back design, creating a Dropbox Paper document which Tim and Diana were able to manage efficiently allowing for information to remain relevant.

IMPACT

A relatively simple solution, the guides led the residents to engage more with the city, increasing social media posts and creating extra promotion for PADA.
